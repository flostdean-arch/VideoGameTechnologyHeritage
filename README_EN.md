# VideoGameTechnologyHeritage

This is an activity to preserve video game technology for the future. / ビデオゲームの技術を未来に残すための活動です。

## 🎮 Purpose of This Repository

This project aims to record and preserve the history and technical heritage of video game development, passing this knowledge to future developers and researchers.

**Specifically:**
- Organize knowledge about past game engines, tools, and algorithms
- Document development techniques for classic hardware (Famicom, Mega Drive, PlayStation, etc.)
- Inherit the history of ingenuity and trial-and-error in game development
- Discover and share old techniques and insights that can be applied to modern development

Video games are not just entertainment, but rather **a practical testbed for many computer science technologies such as graphics, AI, networking, and physics simulation.** This repository serves as a bridge between gaming history and technological innovation.

---

## 📝 What We're Looking For

We welcome the following types of content and contributions:

- **Game Technology Articles & Documentation**
  - How retro gaming systems worked and their constraints
  - Development technique explanations for famous games
  - Introduction of algorithms and programming hacks

- **Source Code & Sample Implementations**
  - Retro-style graphics rendering implementations
  - Re-implementations of old architectures
  - Code examples of game development techniques

- **Historical Materials & References**
  - Records of developer interviews and roundtable discussions
  - Industry news and milestones
  - References to technical books and papers

- **Translations & Localization**
  - Japanese translations of English technical documents
  - Translations of Japanese resources into other languages

- **Discussion & Exchange of Ideas**
  - Questions like "How does that technology work?"
  - Technical discussions about game development

## 🤝 Everyone is Welcome to Participate!

**No special qualifications or experience are required to participate in this project.**

### We're looking forward to contributions from:

✅ **Game Development Professionals** - Those who want to share their knowledge and experience  
✅ **Game Enthusiasts** - Those who want to discuss the technology behind their favorite games  
✅ **Programmers & Engineers** - Those interested in implementing old technologies  
✅ **Students & Researchers** - Those who want to learn about game technology  
✅ **Writers & Translators** - Those who want to contribute through article writing and translation  
✅ **Gaming History Fans** - Those who want to document video game industry history  

### How to Participate:

1. **Create an Issue** - Propose topics you'd like to add or ask questions
2. **Submit a Pull Request** - Post articles, code, or materials
3. **Discuss in Discussions** - Exchange ideas and opinions
4. **Edit the Wiki** - Add articles to the knowledge base

**Any form of contribution is welcome. If you're even slightly interested, feel free to get involved!**
